Vision-Language grounding on a Jetson Orin Nano 8 GB — every one of 300 queries

Small (2–3 B) quantized open models, running on the device, no fine-tuning, asked to box an object in aerial imagery. This page shows every query: what was asked, the ground-truth box (green), and what each model drew. The point of the study is not high accuracy — it is where and why these models fail under an 8 GB budget.

How to read the numbers. A “hit” means IoU ≥ 0.5 (a tight box). Accuracy here is modest by design — that is the finding. Two things dominate the misses: (1) the failure is object selection, not box precision — models often draw a clean box around the wrong object; and (2) viewpoint matters a lot — nadir (top-down, satellite-like) is far easier than oblique (drone angle), where the satellite-trained specialists (TinyRS) fall to near-zero because they are off-domain. Use the “right area (0.25–0.5)” filter to see the many near-hits that a strict 0.5 cutoff calls “miss”.
